Here are some recent legislative activities related to gun laws and control as of October 2023:

1. **Federal Firearms Legislation Overview**:
   - A summary document of federal firearms laws was released, detailing frequently encountered federal firearm offenses. This document specifies definitions and regulations concerning firearms. [Read more here](https://www.justice.gov/d9/2023-12/summary_of_federal_firearms_laws_-_october_2023.pdf).

2. **State-Level Gun Law Changes**:
   - In 2023, significant state-level legislation was introduced and passed focusing on gun control. The states most active in introducing restrictive gun legislation included New York, Illinois, and Texas, while Colorado, Maryland, and Washington passed the most firearm control laws. [Read more about these changes](https://www.billtrack50.com/info/blog/important-gun-law-changes-in-2023/).

3. **Connecticut Gun Control Legislation (H.B. 6667)**:
   - Connecticut enacted its most sweeping gun control law since the Sandy Hook shooting. This new legislation includes a broad spectrum of controls and regulations that gun owners need to comply with. [Further details here](https://www.npr.org/2023/10/01/1202888643/connecticut-enacts-its-most-sweeping-gun-control-law-since-the-sandy-hook-shooti).

4. **S.3407 - Gun Violence Prevention and Community Safety Act of 2023**:
   - This Senate bill, introduced by Sen. Elizabeth Warren, aims to expand comprehensive measures to prevent gun violence and ensure community safety. The bill has been referred to the Senate Finance Committee as of December 2023. [Read the full bill details here](https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/senate-bill/3407).

These actions reflect ongoing legislative efforts to address gun control at both federal and state levels. The focus appears to be on enhancing safety and oversight of gun ownership and distribution.
